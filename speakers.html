This year, the main theme of the event will be «Hidden» (Oculto in spanish). We have approached this concept from different perspectives: recovering historic figures, work processes that remains in the shadows, innovacions that step towards the future and new ways of undestanding type. Suming it up, all that isn´t bright and shinny but that at the same time is key in typographic design.

Jean François Porchez
Typefaces as strategic point of passage.

Typography has become a natural extension of the visual identity of the most diverse organizations, governments and businesses, for their publications and products. Typeface design is at a pivotal point in its history, thanks to new creative technologies and uses. Letters and by extension, typographic signs, have always been witness to the conveyance of thoughts between author and reader. Nowadays, the typeset text is not only tied to the world of the books. Since the industrial revolution and the globalization of education, typography has become the gateway to texts and contents, and is a key element of communication between people. We have reached a cross-roads which bears discussion.

Dave Crossland

I attended the University of Reading’s MA Typeface Design programme and graduated in 2009. In my thesis I related the history of the software freedom movement to key concepts in type design. My student project “Cantarell” was included in the launch of Google Web Fonts and chosen as the default User Interface font for GNOME 3. In 2013 I am consulting for the Google Web Fonts project in England, commissioning new typefaces designed for the web, and offering lectures on typeface design with free software. I believe that anyone can learn to draw, that CouchSurfing is the best way to travel, and that Transition Towns is important.

Noe Blanco
Beyond the screen

This project is integrated inside “Milenaria”, a typographic research project that began in 2013 as a creative iniciative linked to the commemoration of a milenium of Granada´s Kingdom.

Rafa Galeano
Tell me what type you have and I´ll tell you how it moves.

Motion arrives into the typography world in order to add character and rythm. A new way of understandind the typographic construction and expand its applications. Nowadays, the relation between the typographer and the animator is getting tighter, achieving an expansion of the typographic uses into other fields like the audiovisual world.
We will see the case of Animopgrahpy and its project Franchise Animated. We will also se other examples of kinetic typography like Garnata Display and exclusively, the animation of Herrería Type.

Manuel Sesma
Spanish typographers pretending to be French

During the middle of the XX Century where three Spanish type designers who worked and achieved some projection in France. All of them made some notorial design works that have passed through the history of French typography. Besides, they participated in different ways to the construction of the theorical basis of the Latin Graphical Symbol´s movement, lead by Maximilien Vox. From those three, Joan Trochut Blanchar and Enric Crous Vidal are perhaps the ones most known, but Guillermo de Mendoza is still, till today, an enigmatic figure.

Penela & Dimas
Richard Gans´s Type Foundry: The twilight of the Gods.

Richard Gans´s foundry was one of the main Spanish lead typography foundries during most of the XX Century. Unfortunately, its path ended on the events-chronicle of newpapers. We will review in this talk, its origins, development and sad end at the same time that we will enjoy its most important typefaces by the hand of his key designer, the German Carl Winkow.

Chema Ribagorda
-

Docente, Tipógrafo y diseñador. Chema is a professor in the Fine Arts School of Complutense University and on the Superior Design School of Madrid, typographer and designer. Currently he has ran a research project about Spanish typography in the XVIII Century, he had concluded Ibarra Real typeface and commisioned an exhibition in the Royal Printing House about the sources of Spanish Typography. Erich Scheichelbauer (Glyphsapp)
Font Gamification: The Power of OpenType

OpenType fonts can do fun stuff. Their built-in glyph substitution tables enable surprising and playful typographic interventions, but can you also put games in fonts? This presentation is a tour de force through OpenType magic, all live, no prefabricated slides. Sit back, relax, and have fun!

Jeroen Krielaars (Animography)
Animated Typography

He will talk about his personal journey from making an animated typeface as an experiment, to setting up a specialized foundry that represents other type/motion designers as well. He have learned a lot in the past few years, both on the design side of things and on the business side of it. It is a constant journey without an endgoal. Nevertheless he always have a clear sense of direction.
